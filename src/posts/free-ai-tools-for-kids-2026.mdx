# Best Free AI Tools for Kids in 2026 (Parent-Tested, Safe to Use)

Every parent knows the moment: your child has discovered an AI tool and is completely absorbed. The question that immediately follows is: is this actually safe? And is it actually free, or are we going to hit a paywall in ten minutes?

We have done the testing. Here is an honest, parent-focused guide to the best free AI tools for children in 2026 — what they do, who they are for, what data they collect, and where the limits are.

## What we looked for

Before recommending any tool, we checked for:

- **Genuine free access** — not just a free trial that expires
- **Age appropriateness** — content filtering and design suited to children
- **Data safety** — COPPA compliance or explicit no-data-collection policies
- **Educational value** — does this actually help a child learn or create something?
- **No account required** — where possible, tools children can use without registering

## 1. Khan Academy — Best for learning with AI tutoring

**Free tier:** Completely free. No ads. No paywall.
**Ages:** 6 and up
**COPPA compliant:** Yes

Khan Academy has integrated AI tutoring across its maths, science, reading, and coding courses. The AI tutor asks guiding questions instead of giving direct answers — which is exactly the right approach for learning.

**What children can do:** Work through structured lessons in maths from basic arithmetic to calculus, science, history, and programming. The AI tutor explains concepts in different ways until the child understands.

**What parents should know:** The parent dashboard shows exactly what your child has been working on and where they are struggling. No advertising. No data selling. This is as trustworthy as it gets.

**Limitation:** The AI tutor requires a free account. The account creation process for children under 13 involves parental consent, which is handled correctly and legally.

## 2. Canva — Best for creative AI projects

**Free tier:** Very generous free tier for personal use. Free for Education is completely free for schools.
**Ages:** 10 and up (younger with supervision)
**COPPA compliant:** Yes for educational accounts

Canva has become the go-to creative tool for schools and children. The free tier includes AI image generation, design templates, presentation tools, and video editing — all in a browser with no download required.

**What children can do:** Design posters, create presentations, generate AI images from text prompts, make short videos, and build portfolios. The interface is genuinely intuitive for children.

**What parents should know:** The free personal plan stores designs in Canva's cloud. The Text to Image AI feature generates images based on descriptions — it has content filters, but adult supervision is still recommended for younger children.

**Limitation:** Some AI features are limited on the free tier. The most powerful tools are in the Pro plan (paid) or the Education plan (free for verified schools and teachers).

## 3. Scratch with AI extensions — Best for learning AI by building it

**Free tier:** Completely free. No account required for basic use.
**Ages:** 8 and up
**COPPA compliant:** Yes

Scratch, the block-based coding platform from MIT, has added AI extensions that let children build their own simple AI projects — image classifiers, voice recognition tools, and machine learning experiments.

**What children can do:** Build a programme that recognises different objects through a webcam. Create a game that responds to voice commands. Train a simple model to distinguish between drawings. These experiences teach how AI actually works, not just how to use it.

**What parents should know:** Scratch accounts for under-13s require parental permission. The community features can be disabled for younger users.

**Limitation:** Scratch AI extensions require some comfort with block coding. It is worth starting with basic Scratch before moving to the AI features.

## 4. Google's AI Experiments — Best for curiosity and exploration

**Free tier:** Completely free. No account required.
**Ages:** All ages (younger ones will need guidance)
**COPPA compliant:** No data collection required

Google's AI Experiments site hosts interactive demos that show how AI works in a fun, hands-on way. No sign-up. No data collection. Just explore.

**Highlights:**
- **Quick Draw** — Draw something and watch AI try to guess what it is in real time. Teaches how AI learns from millions of examples.
- **AutoDraw** — Start a sketch and AI suggests finished drawings that match your scribbles. Wonderful for younger children who feel they cannot draw.
- **Teachable Machine** — Train your own image or sound classifier in the browser. Shows children exactly how machine learning works.

**What parents should know:** These tools do not require accounts and do not collect personal data. They are safe for all ages, though the concepts may need explanation for younger children.

**Limitation:** These are demos, not full applications. They are best used as learning experiences alongside a conversation about how AI works.

## 5. Duolingo — Best for language learning with AI

**Free tier:** Generous free tier with all core content accessible.
**Ages:** 4 and up (the app is designed for young learners)
**COPPA compliant:** Yes — explicit children's privacy protections

Duolingo uses AI to adapt lesson difficulty to each learner and has a purpose-built kids mode for younger children. It is one of the most polished examples of AI personalisation done right for children.

**What children can do:** Learn a new language through short, gamified lessons. The AI adjusts which concepts to review based on what they are getting wrong. Progress is visible and rewarding.

**What parents should know:** Duolingo Kids mode removes social features and leaderboards. The adult version has social elements that are less appropriate for younger children — make sure to use the right version.

**Limitation:** The free tier includes ads in the adult version (not in the Kids version). Some features are locked behind Duolingo Super (the paid plan), but the free tier is genuinely useful.

## Tools to approach with caution

These tools have value but come with significant caveats for children:

**ChatGPT (free tier):** Requires users to be 13 and older. Not designed for children. No parental controls. Better alternatives exist for under-16s.

**Character.AI:** Popular with teenagers for chatting with AI personas. Has faced criticism for emotional attachment risks. Not appropriate for under-13s without close parental involvement.

**Perplexity AI:** A powerful AI search tool, free to use. Minimum age 13. Useful for research but can return adult content in some searches. Appropriate for older teenagers with guidance.

## The honest summary

The best free AI tools for children are the ones built specifically with children in mind. Khan Academy, Canva, Scratch, and Google's AI Experiments are all designed with education as the primary goal — not engagement metrics or data collection.

When in doubt, use this checklist: Is it COPPA compliant? Does it have parental controls? Does it explain rather than just answer? Is there a clear privacy policy?

If the answer to all four is yes, you are in good shape.
