# Is AI Safe for Kids? What Every Parent Needs to Know in 2026

If you've found yourself wondering whether AI is safe for your child — you're not alone. A recent study found that **96% of parents have received zero guidance from their child's school** on how to handle AI. That leaves most families navigating one of the biggest technology shifts of our lifetime without a map.

This guide gives you that map. No jargon. No panic. Just the facts you need.

## What "AI for kids" actually means

When people talk about AI for kids, they're usually talking about two very different things:

1. **General-purpose AI tools** like ChatGPT, Gemini, or Copilot — built for adults, widely used by children anyway
2. **Purpose-built children's AI** — designed from the ground up with age-appropriate content, data protection, and parental controls

The safety conversation looks very different depending on which type you're discussing.

## The honest answer: it depends on the tool

Generic AI chatbots carry real risks for children:
- They can generate inappropriate content if prompted incorrectly
- They collect and may retain conversation data
- They are not age-verified
- They can produce confident-sounding but incorrect information

Purpose-built children's AI platforms address these risks directly — with content filtering, COPPA compliance, closed AI systems, and parent dashboards.

## What COPPA means and why it matters

COPPA — the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act — is the US law that governs how apps collect data from children under 13. Any AI tool your child uses should clearly state COPPA compliance.

What COPPA-compliant means in practice:
- The app cannot collect personal data from children without verified parental consent
- It cannot target advertising at children
- It must have a clear, readable privacy policy
- Parents can request deletion of their child's data

**AvenirCore is built COPPA-compliant from day one.** No ads to children. No data selling. Ever.

## 5 questions to ask before your child uses any AI tool

1. **Does it have a minimum age requirement?** If it says 13+, your 8-year-old shouldn't be using it alone.
2. **Is it COPPA compliant?** Check the privacy policy. It should say so clearly.
3. **Does it show ads?** Advertising-funded AI is not designed with your child's interests first.
4. **Can you see what your child is doing?** Parent dashboards and activity visibility are non-negotiable.
5. **Who built it?** General-purpose AI companies vs. teams focused specifically on child safety are very different.
